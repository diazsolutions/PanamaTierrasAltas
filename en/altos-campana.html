Altos de Campana

The first national park in Panama, where the mountains meet the sea.

Located just an hour from Panama City, Altos de Campana National Park is a natural treasure that offers some of the most spectacular views in the country. Founded in 1966, it is the oldest national park in Panama and serves as a vital biological corridor.

The park is famous for its dramatic cliffs and the Caja de Agua lookout, from where you can appreciate the mouth of the Chame River and the Pacific Ocean in all its splendor. Its cool climate and cloud forest make it a perfect refuge for those seeking a quick escape from the city heat.

Main Activities

Hiking

Trails like "La Cruz" offer a challenging hike with a rewarding 360-degree view at the summit.

Bird Watching

Home to unique species like the orange-bellied trogon and the scale-crested pygmy tyrant.

Photography

The viewpoints offer the best panoramic shots of the Panamanian Pacific coast.

Nature Study

Observe the transition between tropical dry forest and cloud forest.

How to get there

From Panama City, take the Inter-American Highway towards the interior. After passing through Capira, you will find the entrance to the town of Chicá on the right. Follow the road up the mountain until you reach the park entrance.
