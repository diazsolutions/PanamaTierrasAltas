Cerro Jefe

The roof of the Panama province, wrapped in clouds and mystery.

Biodiversity in the capital's heights

Cerro Jefe is the highest mountain in the Panama province, located within the Chagres National Park. It is a destination of scientific and ecological importance, home to numerous endemic species of flora and fauna. Its cloud forest offers a cool and misty respite very close to Panama City, being a favorite place for scientists and birdwatchers.

Main Activities

  • Endemic birdwatching
  • Cloud forest hiking
  • Botanical and scientific research
  • Mountain landscape photography

Relevant Data

Cerro Jefe is vital for the Panama Canal watershed, as its forests help regulate the water cycle in the region.
